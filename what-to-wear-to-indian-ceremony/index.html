What to Wear to Indian Ceremony: A Guide to Traditional Attire

Attending an Indian ceremony can be an exciting experience, but figuring out what to wear can be a bit daunting. You want to dress appropriately and respectfully, but also want to feel comfortable and confident in your outfit. If you're feeling a bit lost on what to wear to an Indian ceremony, don't worry, we've got you covered.

Indian ceremonies are known for their vibrant colors, intricate designs, and traditional attire. Depending on the type of ceremony, the dress code can vary. However, the general rule of thumb is to wear traditional Indian clothing. This can include sarees, salwar kameez, lehenga choli, or anarkali suits for women, and kurta pajama or sherwani for men.

In addition to traditional Indian attire, it's important to keep in mind the modesty of the attire. This means avoiding outfits that are too revealing or tight-fitting. It's also important to wear comfortable shoes, as Indian ceremonies can often be lengthy affairs.

To sum it up, when it comes to what to wear to an Indian ceremony, traditional Indian attire that is modest and comfortable is the way to go. Let's dive deeper into each piece of traditional Indian clothing.

Sarees

Sarees are a popular choice for women attending Indian ceremonies. A saree is a long piece of fabric that is draped around the body and comes in a variety of materials, colors, and designs. It is usually paired with a blouse and petticoat. Sarees can be elegant and sophisticated, making them a great choice for formal events.

When choosing a saree, consider the type of ceremony you'll be attending. For more traditional ceremonies, opt for a silk saree with intricate designs. For a more modern ceremony, you can choose a lighter fabric with simpler designs. Pair your saree with statement jewelry and a clutch bag to complete the look.

Salwar Kameez

Salwar kameez is another popular choice for women attending Indian ceremonies. It consists of a long tunic top (kameez) paired with loose pants (salwar) and a scarf (dupatta). This outfit is comfortable and versatile, making it a great choice for all types of ceremonies.

When choosing a salwar kameez, look for vibrant colors and intricate embroidery. You can opt for a fitted or loose-fitting top, depending on your preference. Pair your salwar kameez with statement earrings and bangles.

Lehenga Choli

Lehenga choli is a traditional Indian outfit that consists of a long skirt (lehenga), a short blouse (choli), and a scarf (dupatta). This outfit is often worn for more formal events, such as weddings and religious ceremonies.

When choosing a lehenga choli, look for rich fabrics, such as silk or velvet, and intricate embroidery. You can choose a skirt with a flared or straight silhouette, depending on your preference. Pair your lehenga choli with statement jewelry and a clutch bag.

Anarkali Suits

Anarkali suits are a modern take on traditional Indian attire. They consist of a long, flowy tunic top paired with fitted pants. This outfit is comfortable and easy to move in, making it a great choice for all types of ceremonies.

When choosing an Anarkali suit, look for vibrant colors and intricate designs. You can opt for a fitted or loose-fitting top, depending on your preference. Pair your Anarkali suit with statement earrings and bangles.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear Western clothing to an Indian ceremony?

A: While it is not recommended, you can wear Western clothing to an Indian ceremony. However, it is important to keep in mind the modesty of the attire and avoid anything too revealing or tight-fitting.

Q: Do I have to wear a saree to an Indian ceremony?

A: No, you don't have to wear a saree to an Indian ceremony. There are a variety of traditional Indian outfits to choose from, such as salwar kameez, lehenga choli, and Anarkali suits.

Q: Can I wear black to an Indian ceremony?

A: Black is generally not worn to Indian ceremonies, as it is considered a color of mourning. Instead, opt for vibrant colors and intricate designs.

Q: Do I have to wear a dupatta with my outfit?

A: It depends on the type of outfit you choose. Sarees and salwar kameez usually come with a dupatta, while lehenga choli and Anarkali suits may or may not come with one. If your outfit doesn't come with a dupatta, you can choose to wear one or not.

Conclusion of What to Wear to Indian Ceremony

Attending an Indian ceremony can be a wonderful experience, and dressing in traditional Indian attire can add to the overall experience. When choosing what to wear to an Indian ceremony, keep in mind the modesty of the attire and opt for something comfortable yet elegant. Whether you choose to wear a saree, salwar kameez, lehenga choli, or Anarkali suit, make sure to pair it with statement jewelry and a clutch bag to complete the look. Happy dressing!
