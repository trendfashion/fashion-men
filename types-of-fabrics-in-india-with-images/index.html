Types Of Fabrics In India With Images

Written by Daniel Aug 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

India is known for its diverse and rich culture, and one aspect of that culture is its textiles. From sarees to kurtas, the fabrics used in traditional Indian clothing are an important part of the country's heritage. However, with so many different types of fabrics available in India, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one. In this post, we'll explore the various types of fabrics in India with images to make it easier for you to make a choice.

When it comes to purchasing fabrics in India, it can be hard to know where to start. With so many different materials, colors, and patterns available, it's easy to get lost in the vast selection. Additionally, there are different types of fabrics that are suitable for different occasions, seasons, and body types. This can make choosing the right fabric a daunting task for anyone, especially those who are new to the world of Indian textiles.

The aim of this post is to provide an overview of the different types of fabrics in India with images, so that you can make an informed decision when purchasing fabric for your next outfit. We'll cover the most popular fabrics used in traditional Indian clothing, as well as some lesser-known options that are worth exploring.

Cotton

Cotton is one of the most popular fabrics in India, and for good reason. It's lightweight, breathable, and perfect for summer wear. Additionally, it's easy to care for and can be machine-washed. Cotton is available in a range of colors and patterns, from simple solids to intricate prints. Some of the most popular types of cotton fabrics in India include:

  • Khadi
  • Mulmul
  • Cambric
  • Voile

Personally, I love wearing cotton sarees during the summer months. They're comfortable, chic, and perfect for the hot and humid weather.

Silk

When it comes to luxury fabrics, silk is at the top of the list. Silk fabrics are known for their lustrous sheen, soft texture, and durability. However, silk can be expensive, and it requires special care. There are many different types of silk fabrics available in India, including:

  • Banarasi silk
  • Kanchipuram silk
  • Tussar silk
  • Chanderi silk

Personally, I love wearing silk sarees for weddings and formal occasions. They're elegant and timeless.

Chiffon

Chiffon is a lightweight and sheer fabric that's perfect for creating flowy and feminine outfits. It's a popular choice for sarees and lehengas, and it's available in a range of colors and prints. Chiffon can be a bit tricky to work with, as it's delicate and can snag easily. However, the end result is worth it.

Brocade

Brocade is a rich and ornate fabric that's perfect for creating elaborate and intricate outfits. It's a woven fabric that features raised designs, and it's often embellished with metallic threads and sequins. Brocade is a popular choice for wedding attire, and it's available in a range of colors and designs.

Conclusion of Types of Fabrics in India with Images

Choosing the right fabric for your next outfit can be overwhelming, especially if you're not familiar with the different types of fabrics available in India. However, by familiarizing yourself with the most popular fabrics and their characteristics, you can make an informed decision and create a beautiful and comfortable outfit that you'll love wearing.
